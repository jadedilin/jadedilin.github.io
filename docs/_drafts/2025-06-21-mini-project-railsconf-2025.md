Introduction 

A steward on the AA0073 from Heathrow to Philadelphia hands me an icecream pot as I set down to write this. I booked a week off my usual freelance projects, I wrapped up my training after a few months of consistently pushing and I'm headed to the USA for the first time to attend RailsConf 2025. I submit this as my mini-project presentation, a requirement of the Scholars and Guides Program.  

The Scholars and Guides Program is a mentorship program for aspiring Rubyists interested in learning more about the Ruby programming language, expanding their professional toolkits, and having help navigating the Ruby Central conferences, RubyConf, and RailsConf. The mini-project is designed to facilitate skill growth and inspire others.



In this post I'll set out to cover my experience at the very last RailsConf as a student of Ruby in the early stages of a career transition. 

My Mini-Project 

The theme of the project this year was *Ruby for Funsies*. For the mini-project my guide Michelle Yuen simulated a working environment in assigning issues in the ChicagoRuby repository for me to solve. By updating the site metadata I enabled the city of Chicago to continue their Ruby related festivities . I created this personal blog for posterity, baby's first deployed site (thanks to GitHub pages). I asked some questions that I needed answers to. And Ruby Central recorded me talking about the experience. Why is my project fragmented into four parts? Because that's how my mind works, and it was more fun that way.
